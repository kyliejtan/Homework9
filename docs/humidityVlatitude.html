Humidity

In regard to the relationship we observed between latitude and humidity, humidity appears to be lowest around 30 degrees latitude in both the northern and southern hemispheres. This is consistent with the latitudes around 30 degrees north and south being the latitudes around which most of the worlds deserts exist due to the subsidence of cool, dry air from the downward legs of the Hadley Cells in either hemisphere. Conversely, we observed that humidity is highest around the equator which can be explained by the dry air that subsides around 30 degrees north and south picking up water as it moves towards the equator under the Coriolis effect. These air masses become more saturated as they move toward the eqautor, and once there they collide, are forced eastward, and rise due to convection. As these air masses rise, they precipitate water because with increased altitude, these air masses are less able to hold water. These factors contribute to the increased humidity in the equatorial region.
